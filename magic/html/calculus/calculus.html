Calculus

Differentiation

Differentiation is the process of finding the derivative of a function, which measures how the function value changes as its input changes.

Key Concepts: Derivative, Slope of a Tangent Line, Rate of Change.

Integration

Integration is the process of finding the integral of a function, which represents the accumulation of quantities and the area under a curve.

Key Concepts: Integral, Area under a Curve, Accumulated Quantity.

Limits

Limits are used to define the behavior of a function as its input approaches a certain value. They are essential in defining derivatives and integrals.

Key Concepts: Limit, Continuity, Infinite Limits.

Series

Series are used to represent functions as sums of simpler functions. They are used in many areas of mathematics and are crucial for approximations.

Key Concepts: Convergence, Power Series, Taylor Series.
